The Bridges of Boone County

According to the National Inventory Bridge Inventory Database, the sufficiency rating is a method of calculating the bridge's sufficiency to remain in service based on four factors. The sufficiency rating is the overall rating of a bridge's fitness for the duty it performs. The number is a percentage where 100 percent would be a completely sufficient bridge and zero would be an entirely insufficient bridge. The bridges built in Boone County since 1905 have an average sufficiency rating of 6.78. The inventory database says that a low sufficiency rating may be caused by "structural defects, narrow lanes, low vertical clearance, or any of many possible issues."
